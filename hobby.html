Core Design Principles
B

Balance

Visual balance gives a design its sense of stability. Symmetry feels formal; asymmetry feels dynamic. Both are valid when used with intention.

C

Contrast

Contrast directs attention. Without it, everything competes and nothing wins. The highest contrast element is always what the eye finds first.

R

Repetition

Repeating colors, shapes, and type builds visual identity. It is what makes a brand recognizable across every touchpoint.

A

Alignment

Nothing should be placed by accident. Alignment creates invisible connections between elements that make a layout feel organized.

P

Proximity

Related items belong together. The space between elements communicates relationship — closeness means connection.

S

Space

White space is not empty — it is breathing room. It gives content clarity and prevents a design from feeling crowded.

Color Theory — Hover to Explore
Primary Colors

hover to learn

Red, Blue, and Yellow are the foundation. They cannot be mixed from any other colors — everything else derives from them.

Secondary Colors

hover to learn

Mix two primaries and you create a secondary color. Red + Yellow = Orange, Blue + Yellow = Green, Red + Blue = Purple.

Warm vs Cool

hover to learn

Warm colors create energy and urgency. Cool colors communicate calm and trust. Both can coexist when balanced thoughtfully.

Color Harmony

hover to learn

Complementary colors (opposite on the wheel) create contrast. Analogous colors (side by side) create cohesion and flow.

My Design Process
01

Research and Discovery

Understand the client, the audience, and the goal. Collect references and define the visual direction before any design work begins.

02

Concept and Sketching

Explore several ideas quickly — on paper first. The best solution rarely comes first; quantity leads to quality.

03

Digital Execution

Bring the strongest concept into the tool. Refine color, type, spacing, and composition until every element feels right.

04

Review and Refine

Step away, then return. Incorporate feedback and improve. Good design is always iterative — rarely perfect on the first pass.

05

Deliver and Document

Export in the correct formats. Document the design system — colors, fonts, spacing — so the work remains consistent over time.
